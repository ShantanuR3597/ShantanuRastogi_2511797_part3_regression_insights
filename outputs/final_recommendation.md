# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

A regression analysis was conducted to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales performance across stores. Two simple regression models and one multiple regression model were evaluated.

The Multiple Regression Model was selected as the final model because it achieved the highest explanatory power with an R² value of 0.857, meaning it explains approximately 85.7% of the variation in monthly sales.

The analysis indicates that customer traffic, marketing investment, inventory availability, staffing levels, customer experience, holiday periods, regional differences, and store format are important drivers of sales performance.

---

# Key Factors Associated with Monthly Sales

The strongest positive factors associated with monthly sales are:

- Footfall
- Marketing Spend
- Inventory Availability Percentage
- Staff Count
- Customer Rating
- Holiday Periods

Additional significant factors include:

- Region South
- Region West
- Store Type High Street
- Store Type Residential

The analysis suggests that stores with higher customer traffic, stronger inventory availability, better customer ratings, and sufficient staffing levels tend to achieve higher sales performance.

# Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

The following variables were not statistically significant in the final model:

- Average Discount Percentage
- Region North
- Store Type Mall

Because these variables were not statistically significant, the model does not provide sufficient evidence that they have a meaningful impact on monthly sales.

Leadership should avoid making major business decisions based solely on these factors.

---

# Risks and Limitations

Several limitations should be considered when interpreting the results:

- Regression analysis identifies associations, not direct cause-and-effect relationships.
- Some business drivers may not be included in the dataset.
- Local economic conditions may influence store performance.
- Competitor promotions and market activity were not fully captured.
- Store management quality and brand reputation were not measured.

These factors may influence sales but are not represented in the model.

---

# Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis measures statistical relationships between variables.

A significant relationship indicates that two variables move together, but it does not automatically prove that one variable directly causes the other.

For example:

- Higher footfall is associated with higher sales.
- However, other factors such as promotions, store quality, seasonality, or local demand may influence both footfall and sales simultaneously.

Therefore, regression should be used as a decision-support tool rather than definitive proof of causality.

---

# Final Recommendation

The Multiple Regression Model should be used as the primary analytical model for understanding monthly sales performance.

Leadership should focus on increasing customer footfall, maintaining inventory availability, optimizing staffing levels, and improving customer experience.

The model explains approximately 85.7% of the variation in monthly sales and provides strong evidence that these operational and customer-related factors are associated with stronger store performance.

While the model provides valuable business insight, future analysis should incorporate additional variables and experimental testing to validate causal relationships before making major strategic investments.
